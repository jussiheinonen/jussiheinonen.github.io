March 20

A new clinical field in dentistry: Oral Clinical Chemistry

Periodontitis, a global health problem affecting almost half of the world’s population, commonly goes unnoticed because of its asymptomatic and pain-free nature. Periodontitis is an inflammatory infectious disease that causes the breakdown of the collagen fibres that attach the teeth to the jawbone. Early signs of periodontitis include red gums, bleeding gums, accumulated dental plaque and bad breath.

Research indicates that 95% of all oral diseases are attributed to uncontrolled plaque accumulation. The harmful bacteria present in plaque can escalate the risk of severe conditions, like diabetes and Alzheimer’s disease.

A new study published 13th March 2025 in the Dentistry Journal discusses oral medicine and oral clinical chemistry game changers for future plaque control and maintenance. The study focuses on Periosafe® aMMP-8 POCT, Lumoral® 2 x PDT and Lingora® Fermented Lingonberry Oral Rinse treatments.

These treatments have shown promising results in previous studies and have the potential to revolutionise diagnostics, prevention, oral care, plaque control and maintenance.

These new oral medicine technologies have launched a new clinical field in dentistry: Oral Clinical Chemistry
